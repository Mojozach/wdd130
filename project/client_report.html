Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?:

For my bathroom site plan, I researched different types of bathrooms around the world and selected high-quality images that clearly represented each type. I chose content based on my personal travel experiences, since I have visited multiple countries and understand how confusing unfamiliar restrooms can be. This helped me create a site that is both informative and relatable. I also used AI tools to revise my writing to improve clarity and grammar.

How did you communicate with your developer? Any issues?:

I communicated with my developers from the beginning by providing clear instructions, expectations, and examples from my site plan. I explained which elements needed to be followed exactly and which areas allowed for creativity, such as layout spacing and color palettes. I regularly checked in during class to answer questions and provide feedback. There were no major communication issues, and feedback cycles were handled smoothly.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?:

Yes, the developer met my expectations and followed the site plan closely. They successfully implemented the structure, layout, and functionality I requested. For example, they correctly built multiple pages and maintained consistent navigation. One improvement area would be refining color choices to better match the overall theme.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team?:

Next time, I would create a more detailed and polished site plan, including clearer design examples and stronger color/font choices. I would also gather feedback from others before handing it off to developers to ensure the design is visually effective and easier to follow.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?:

As the lead developer, I started by setting up the project structure, including the homepage, navigation, and shared CSS files. This allowed junior developers to follow a consistent format. I assigned each developer a specific page and explained the requirements clearly. I also used file organization and naming conventions to prevent confusion. For example, I matched CSS files to their corresponding HTML pages and documented responsibilities in the README.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?:

I communicated with the client by asking clarifying questions about design choices such as colors, layout, and required content. I confirmed which parts of the design were flexible and which were required. After completing key parts of the site, I shared updates and gathered feedback to ensure the project stayed aligned with expectations. Communication was consistent, and no major issues occurred.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?:

I used GitHub to manage and publish the project. I ensured all team members had proper access and that the repository was organized before publishing. After finalizing the project, I deployed the site using GitHub Pages and shared the live link with the client. I also verified that all links and assets worked correctly on the hosted version.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?:

My experience with GitHub was positive overall. I used GitHub Desktop to commit and push updates regularly, which helped maintain version control. One challenge I encountered was fixing broken links that worked locally but not on the live site. I learned that this was due to incorrect file paths, and fixing those improved my understanding of project structure and deployment.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?:

I communicated with my team leads during class by asking about repository setup, permissions, and assigned tasks. I made sure I understood which page I was responsible for and asked questions when instructions were unclear. One challenge I faced was that one team lead didn't write anything, which delayed communication and reduced development time. In those cases, I helped out the team lead to set up the basic to allow the junior developers to start.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?:

In some projects, expectations were clear and well-communicated. In others, instructions were limited, so I had to analyze the site plan and existing pages to determine what needed to be done. For example, I helped set up parts of the homepage in one project so I could proceed with my assigned page that I believed I had.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?:

Yes, I contributed to all assigned sites. I completed pages such as the “Tips and Tricks” section for one project and created a collage-style image page for "Corgi.com". In one case, I took on additional responsibility similar to a lead developer by helping organize the layout and structure when instructions were unclear.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?:

The main issues I encountered were related to file paths and responsive design. Some links worked locally but failed on the live site, which required debugging. I also encountered a case where a navigation link was missing, which affected usability. These challenges helped me improve my problem-solving skills.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?:

The most challenging part was working with layout techniques such as CSS Grid and positioning elements correctly. For example, I had difficulty placing logos and aligning content as intended in the design. I also struggled with file pathing for images and links. I improved by seeking help from TAs and using debugging strategies, which strengthened my understanding of HTML and CSS.

What did you enjoy most about this project?:

I enjoy see what other people are interested in and the differences in layouts people put for their wireframes for their site plans. One of the site plans I worked on was from a girl from japan and her site plan was about visiting japan and what is needed to know. For me I always wanted to visit Japan and this help me to prep in material and knowledge on what to do. The other site plan I learned someone love the mod for Terraria and other one like a specific type of corgi. Doing these were fun and cool to see what someone like and interest are.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?:

I would made my site plan a lot cleaner to make it more appealing to viewers. I would practice more of grid movement and boxing to do these site plans better or with less help from TA or ai. Overall I had learned a lot about web development.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience?:

Yes and no. If I had to work from the start with no help in any kind I would not feel comfortable working in real- world projects. If I can have help from a team or ai I would say I can manage. I would say I couldn't do very complex site plan like animination but a basic site plan I believe I could do it in the real- world.
