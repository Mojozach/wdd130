Yes, the developer met the expectations of my site plan perfectly. I know there was some hard coding challenges in it but they executed the coding perfectly to the site plan I have given them. The only issues I had was maybe making better choices on color pallets.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?:

I first set up the home page with all the basic parts like a header, footer, and links to other pages to allow the junior developers to copy and edit. I also added images such as logo to help a little bit. I also met with each developer and discussed which page they would work on and what the requirement of it. In coding I tried to match which style files match with html files and in the read me specify which page each developer is working on.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?:

I asked her any qustion such as how does she want the color pallets on her site plan to look and understand which part is up to us and which part is specifical designed. After I finished up my part I check with the client to see what her thoughts were and if they any changes she wants.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?:

I handled publishing my client's site and sharing it in a repectful and controled way. I made sure that the only authorized people that was part of it had the git to edit and to see the project. I made sure there isn't any personal information leak in the edits and site plan. After I was finished with it I sent throught the same email the link for the client to view the site and see how well it was done.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?:

I would say managing and my experience were pretty easy. Using Git desktop to commit and push to origin any coding made to the repository was simple and easy. The only challenge I had was making sure the links worked on the git hub link to site because it would work on my computer and later found out it was a slash error or location error.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?:

The most challenging parts of these projects I would say is doing placement of images and text boxes because this involes grid work and I have not been the best at this. Like for the example of the Calmity site plan the logo supposed to be on the left side of the title but it located on top. The other issues I had was with pathing of links with images or even to styles. I ran into this issue many times and always needed to jump deep into the pathing to understand and if stuck for too long asked ai to see if it can spot the error and provide a way to fix it. I overcome some of the text box and image placement with TA and ai but for the logo one I jsut didn't have enough time to fix it.

What did you enjoy most about this project?:

I enjoy see what other people are interested in and the differences in layouts people put for their wireframes for their site plans. One of the site plans I worked on was from a girl from japan and her site plan was about visiting japan and what is needed to know. For me I always wanted to visit Japan and this help me to prep in material and knowledge on what to do. The other site plan I learned someone love the modded version of Terraria and other one like a specific type of corgi. Doing these were fun and cool to see what someone like and interest are.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?:

I would made my site plan a lot cleaner to make it more appealing to viewers. I would practice more of grid movement and boxing to do these site plans better or with less help from TA or ai. Overall I had learned a lot about web development.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience?:

Yes and no. If I had to work from the start with no help in any kind I would not feel comfortable working in real- world projects. If I can have help from a team or ai I would say I can manage. I would say I couldn't do very complex site plan like animination but a basic site plan I believe I could do it in the real- world.
